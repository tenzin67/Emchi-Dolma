## The Blueprint of Your Being

In Tibetan Medicine, every human being arrives in this world with a unique constitutional blueprint — a particular balance of three fundamental energies called the **Nyepas** (ཉེས་པ་). Understanding your dominant Nyepa is like receiving a map of your inner landscape: it explains why you fall ill in particular ways, why certain seasons affect you more than others, and which foods, behaviors, and therapies will most powerfully restore your wellbeing.

Unlike Western medicine's one-size-fits-all approach, Tibetan Medicine begins with the radical acknowledgment that every person is different — and must be treated as such.

## Rlung (རླུང་) — The Wind Energy

**Rlung** (pronounced "Loong") is the energy of movement, circulation, and subtle communication within the body. It governs:

- Breathing and the movement of air
- The nervous system and sensory experience
- Mental activity, thought, and creativity
- The flow of blood, lymph, and vital energies
- Speech and vocal expression

### Rlung Body Type
People with dominant Rlung tend to be creative, enthusiastic, and quick-thinking. They are often slender with dry skin, prone to cold, and intensely responsive to their environment. When balanced, they are visionaries and communicators. When imbalanced, they suffer from anxiety, insomnia, restlessness, racing thoughts, and joint pain.

### What Disturbs Rlung
- Cold, rough, and light foods (raw salads, dried foods, cold drinks)
- Irregular sleep patterns and erratic eating
- Excessive talking, worry, and multitasking
- Grief, fear, and emotional volatility
- Autumn and winter seasons

### Restoring Rlung Balance
- Warm, oily, nourishing foods (soups, stews, ghee)
- Regular routine and adequate sleep
- Warm oil massage (Kanye therapy)
- Grounding practices: meditation, walking in nature
- Gentle warmth and quietude

## Tripa (མཁྲིས་པ་) — The Bile Energy

**Tripa** (pronounced "Tri-pa") is the energy of transformation, heat, and intelligence. It governs:

- Digestion and metabolism
- Body temperature and the generation of heat
- Vision and the processing of light
- Intellectual clarity and decisiveness
- Courage and determination

### Tripa Body Type
Tripa-dominant individuals are often medium-build with warm skin, good appetite, and sharp minds. They are natural leaders — passionate, driven, and precise. When imbalanced, they become prone to inflammation, anger, heartburn, skin rashes, and liver conditions.

### What Disturbs Tripa
- Hot, spicy, oily, and sharp foods
- Alcohol and fermented foods
- Excessive sun exposure and hot environments
- Anger, frustration, and competitive pressure
- Summer season

### Restoring Tripa Balance
- Cooling, bitter, and sweet foods (leafy greens, dairy, sweet fruits)
- Moderation in exercise and work intensity
- Cooling therapies and gentle purgation
- Cultivating patience and compassion
- Exposure to cool environments

## Béken (བད་ཀན་) — The Phlegm Energy

**Béken** (pronounced "Beh-gen") is the energy of stability, lubrication, and nourishment. It governs:

- Fluid balance throughout the body
- Lubrication of joints and organs
- Sleep and restorative processes
- Memory and mental steadiness
- Structural integrity of tissues

### Béken Body Type
Béken-dominant individuals are often solidly built with smooth, moist skin and calm temperaments. They are patient, loyal, and methodical. When balanced, they are pillars of stability. When imbalanced, they suffer from lethargy, weight gain, congestion, poor digestion, diabetes, and depression.

### What Disturbs Béken
- Cold, heavy, sweet, and oily foods
- Excess sleep and sedentary lifestyle
- Cold and damp environments
- Attachment and inertia
- Winter and spring seasons

### Restoring Béken Balance
- Light, warming, and pungent foods
- Regular vigorous exercise
- Dry and stimulating therapies (dry massage, sauna)
- Fasting and detoxification
- Mental stimulation and new challenges
